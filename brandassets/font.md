# Typography

## Typefaces

| Role       | Typeface                    | Weight  | Use                        |
|------------|-----------------------------|---------|----------------------------|
| Primary    | ITC Elan Pro                | Book    | Headlines                  |
| Supporting | Helvetica Now Display       | Light / Regular | Sub-headlines, body, detail |

---

## Primary Typeface — ITC Elan Pro

**Used for:** Headlines only.

**Justification:** Centred is the preferred justification. When centre justification doesn't suit the layout (e.g. digital ads or homepage banners), left-justified is the next best option.

**Leading:** Tight. Subtract 4 pt from the font size.
> Example: 32 pt type → 28 pt leading

**Kerning:** Tight. ITC Elan Pro Book requires extra attention to create a consistent perceived space between letterforms, producing a bold, graphic quality.

---

## Supporting Typeface — Helvetica Now Display

**Used for:** Sub-headlines, eyebrows, body copy, and detail text.

**Justification:** Centre-justified for sub-headlines and eyebrows. Left-justified for body copy.

**Leading:** Spacious. Add 4 pt to the font size.
> Example: 16 pt type → 20 pt leading

---

## Type Scale & Guidance

### Headlines
- **Typeface:** ITC Elan Pro Book
- **Case:** All caps
- **Sizes — Digital:** 28 pt – 58 pt
- **Sizes — Print:** 14 pt – 28 pt
- **Line Height:** 4 pt less than type size

**Usage notes:**
- Set in a confident, utilitarian style reminiscent of a scientific journal — credible and authoritative.
- Headlines should never be large; emphasis comes from surrounding white space.
- Written in as few words as possible.
- May be paired with a secondary headline as a typographic lockup in more editorial contexts.

---

### Sub-headlines
- **Typeface:** Helvetica Now Display Regular
- **Case:** All caps
- **Size:** 25–75% of the headline height (never the same size)
- **Line Height:** 4 pt larger than text size

**Usage notes:**
- Small, confident, and utilitarian — emphasised with space.
- Alignment varies: centred for hierarchy, left-aligned for body-adjacent layouts.
- May extend to two lines when affixed to the top or bottom of a composition.

---

### Body Copy
- **Typeface:** Helvetica Now Display Regular
- **Case:** Sentence case
- **Sizes — Print:** 12 pt – 16 pt
- **Sizes — Digital:** 14 pt – 18 pt
- **Line Height:** 4 pt larger than text size
- **Alignment:** Left-aligned

**Usage notes:**
- Maximum 50–60 characters per line; roughly 300 characters per paragraph.
- Organised using a Swiss typographic grid system — consistent spacing and flush-left alignment.
- May be paired with a section header, introduction, and/or pull quotes to create content blocks.
- **Accessibility:** No smaller than 12 pt on mobile, 14 pt on desktop.

---

### Detail (Eyebrows, Captions, Footnotes, Legal, Location Tags)
- **Typeface:** Helvetica Now Display — Regular, Italic, and Bold
- **Case:** Sentence case
- **Sizes — Print:** 8 pt – 12 pt
- **Sizes — Digital:** 12 pt – 16 pt
- **Line Height:** 4 pt larger than text size

**Usage notes:**
- Positioned towards the edges of an image or composition.
- **Accessibility:** No smaller than 12 pt on mobile, 10 pt on desktop.

---

## Type Pairing Principle

Typography should be paired and set to establish a clear hierarchy while maintaining an editorial feel. Our typography is utilitarian by nature.
